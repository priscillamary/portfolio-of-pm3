Purpose

This is what my life really looks like, and although past-me used to be more "well-rounded", past-me would worry about anything and everything. Past-me also hadn't found her interest in Computer Science yet... - all the more reason to enjoy the start of my programming journey! I got into coding when I first started Girls Who Code at JP Morgan Chase & Co. here in Jersey City, NJ as a part of the Summer Immersion Program. I absolutely love the program, the other girls, the teaching environment, the culture of JPMC, and most importantly - the compsci of it all. I cannot begin to say, really, how much this program has turned my whole life around in a whole 360. I now have opportunities and priceless connections/information with/from GWC girls, instructors, and speakers. I want to share my progress and personality with you all through this website - a grand project containing smaller ones. "What My Life Really Looks Like" is a reality I can say I have no worries about, but will not accept as enough. An "ideal life" would mean everything is equal in personal significance and meaning, hence a balanced life, but is this really the ideal life? I don't think so. In an "ideal" world, everything would be meaningless and have no weight/substance BECAUSE of the equal importance. As a student, even I know "ideal" is impossible to achieve, but I guess I've learned to priortize and purposely be great at one thing that I absolutely love rather than being well-rounded. I guess I'm just always thinking about ways to improve myself and help others (in that order). That "hungry" mindset, I can say, is the only thing I accept. I'm simply a work in progress...
